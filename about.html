About Verb Noun

A critical examination of design as cultural practice, exploring the intersection of form and meaning in contemporary discourse.

Our Mission

VerbNoun is a literary magazine dedicated to exploring the intersection of language, meaning, and human experience. We believe that words are not just symbols on a page, but living entities that shape our understanding of the world.

Our name reflects our core philosophy: verbs represent action, movement, and change, while nouns ground us in the concrete realities of existence. Together, they form the foundation of all meaningful communication.

What We Publish

We seek writing that challenges conventional thinking, explores complex themes, and demonstrates mastery of craft. Our pages feature:

  • Original essays that examine contemporary issues through a literary lens
  • Fiction that pushes boundaries and explores new narrative possibilities
  • Poetry that captures the essence of human experience
  • Interviews with writers, thinkers, and cultural commentators
  • Reviews of books that matter in our literary landscape

Contributors

Dr. Elena Vasquez

Contributing Editor & Cultural Critic

Dr. Vasquez holds a PhD in Comparative Literature from Columbia University and has written extensively on the intersection of language, power, and cultural identity. Her essays on contemporary media and literary criticism have appeared in The Paris Review, n+1, and Cabinet Magazine. She currently teaches at the New School and serves as cultural editor for several independent publications.

Photo by Thought Catalog

Marcus Klein

Design Philosophy Writer

Marcus is a practicing architect and writer whose work explores the philosophical underpinnings of design thinking. His background spans both the Bauhaus tradition and contemporary digital culture, bringing a unique perspective to questions of form, function, and meaning. He has contributed to Architectural Review, Domus, and various design criticism platforms while maintaining his practice in Berlin.

Photo by Rachel McDermott

Maya Chen

Aesthetic Theory & Technology Writer

Maya brings a background in both computer science and aesthetic theory to her writing on technology and culture. With an MFA from Yale and professional experience in user experience design, she examines how digital interfaces shape human perception and social interaction. Her work has been featured in Real Life Magazine, Logic, and The New Inquiry, focusing on the political implications of design decisions.

Colophon

Verb Noun is set in Caladea, a serif typeface designed by Carolina Giovagnoli and Andrés Torresi for Huerta Tipográfica. The site is built with Jekyll and hosted on GitHub Pages.

The name reflects our belief that language operates through the dynamic relationship between action (verb) and object (noun), movement and substance, process and form.
