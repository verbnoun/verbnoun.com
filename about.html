About Verb Noun

A critical examination of design as cultural practice, exploring the intersection of form and meaning in contemporary discourse.

Our Mission

VerbNoun is a literary magazine dedicated to exploring the intersection of language, meaning, and human experience. We believe that words are not just symbols on a page, but living entities that shape our understanding of the world.

Our name reflects our core philosophy: verbs represent action, movement, and change, while nouns ground us in the concrete realities of existence. Together, they form the foundation of all meaningful communication.

What We Publish

We seek writing that challenges conventional thinking, explores complex themes, and demonstrates mastery of craft. Our pages feature:

  • Original essays that examine contemporary issues through a literary lens
  • Fiction that pushes boundaries and explores new narrative possibilities
  • Poetry that captures the essence of human experience
  • Interviews with writers, thinkers, and cultural commentators
  • Reviews of books that matter in our literary landscape

Colophon

Verb Noun is set in Caladea, a serif typeface designed by Carolina Giovagnoli and Andrés Torresi for Huerta Tipográfica. The site is built with Jekyll and hosted on GitHub Pages.

The name reflects our belief that language operates through the dynamic relationship between action (verb) and object (noun), movement and substance, process and form.
